Mendelssohn Violin Concerto

Felix Mendelssohn's Violin Concerto in E minor, Op. 64, is his last large orchestral work.

It forms an important part of the violin repertoire and is one of the most popular and most frequently performed violin concertos in history.[1][2][3] A typical performance lasts just under half an hour.
